Exercise Tips - Bicep Curl

Synopsis

The bicep curl is an upper-body weight training exercise meant to strengthen the bicep and other muscles that interact
with the elbow, such as the brachialis and the brachioradialis. It is one of the most common exercises that people do
to build arm strength and definition.

The Basics

Bicep curls are relatively simple to do, but it is important to have proper form when you do them.
If you go too fast, or swing the barbells with momentum, you won't be able to receive the benefits as
if you performed the exercise properly.
  1. Select a weight that you think would be maintanable for around 10 reps
  2. Stand with your feet about shoulder-width apart
  3. Face your palms outwards with a weight in each hand and ensure your elbows are pressed to your ribs
  4. While keeping everything else still, curl the weights upwards towards your shoulders
  5. At the top of the curl, ensure you get good contraction in your biceps
  6. Slowly lower the weights back to the starting position
  7. Repeat this lifting for your number of reps to make a total of 3 sets

Variations

There are many variations on the bicep curl, which are including but not limited to:
  • Standing curls: Curls done while standing
  • Sitting curls: Curls done while sitting on a bench that lets you hang your arms to the side
  • Preacher curls: Using a preacher bench to facilitate curls. You rest your arms on the decline and curl
  • Reverse curls: Doing bicep curls with your palms facing downwards
  • Concentration curls: Doing curls with one at a time to really focus on the muscle
