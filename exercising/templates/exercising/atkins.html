Diet Tips - Atkins

Synopsis

The atkins diet is one that focuses on cutting carbohydrates rather than other macronutrients like fat.
In fact, by reducing carbohydrates, you must somewhat increase your intake of fats and proteins.
This helps cut out many refined carbohydrates and sugars that can be found in our grain products such as bread or pasta.
While some diets may encourage calorie counting as a means of measuring food intake, this diet focuses on
carbohydrate counting. This diet uniquely has phases that you progress through while you're on it.
Lastly, while it seems familiar to the keto diet, it differs because it doesn't create a limit on protein consumption
and it allows you to eventually introduce carbohydrates back into your diet.

The Phases of the Diet:

  1. Induction: The strictest phase of the diet where you should only eat 20 grams of carbohydrates that derive from vegetables.
    You will be eating consuming mostly protein and fat in this phase and cannot have foods that are asscoaited with carbohydrates.
  2. Balancing: In this phase, you should still be consuming the majority of your carbohydrates from vegetables, but you can
    resume eating some carbohydrates found in nutrient-rich foods. This should be until you are 10 pounds above your goal weight.
  3. Pre-maintenance: Here, you are again able to have a wider range of foods including fruits and whole grains, where your
    total intake of carbohydrates increases by about 10 grams a week. This continues until you reach your goal weight.
  4. Lifetime maintenance: In the final phase, you are at your goal weight and can eat the amount of carbohydrates that allows
    you to stay at your goal weight indefinitely.

Effects:

Some may find that the atkins diet works for them because by reducing the amount of carbs they eat, they reduce the amount of calories
they eat overall in a day. Additionally, the increase in protein intake may make your feel fuller throughout the day and less likely to eat.
It's important to note that some of the risks associated with the diet can also be found in the keto diet. Low intake of carbohydrates can lead
to ketosis, which can cause headaches, dizziness, weakness, fatigue, or constipation.
