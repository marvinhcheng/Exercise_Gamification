Diet Tips - Vegetarian

Synopsis

The vegetarian diet is useful for those who do not want to eat meat for religious or ethical reasons,
but also for various health benefits. As it sounds, the general diet is limited to vegetables and animal products only.

The Basics

The vegetarian diet's defining feature is abstination from eating meat, poultry, or fish. However, this does allow for the
inclusion of animal products in the diet, which is veganism. An important detail of the vegetarian diet is the planning of meals.
Certain vitamins and minerals that are common in meats, but less so in vegetarian foods need to be included in your diet to prevent nutrient deficits.
Some of the more likely deficiencies are Vitamin B-12, iron, omega-3 fatty acids, and certain essential amino acids usually found in animal protein.

Variations

  • Pescatarian: Excludes meats and poultry, but allows for fish and occasionally eggs/dairy
  • Ovo-vegetarian: Excludes meat, poultry, fish, and dairy but allows for eggs
  • Lacto-vegetarian: Excludes meat, poultry, fish, and eggs but allows for dairy
  • Lacto-ovo-vegetarian: Excludes meat, poultry, fish, but allows for both eggs and dairy
  • Veganism: Strictly excludes meat, poultry, fish, eggs, and dairy. This is the most restrictive of the diets
    and is centered around consuming no animal products whatsoever
