Exercise Tips - Bench Press

Synopsis

The bench press is an upper-body weight training exercise in which the trainee presses a weight upwards while lying on a weight training bench.
The exercise uses the pectoralis major, the anterior deltoids, and the triceps, among other stabilizing muscles.
A barbell is generally used to hold the weight, but a pair of dumbbells can also be used.

The Basics

The bench press requires a few pieces of equipment. You will need either a barbell or dumbbells and a flat bench.
Form is always important for exercises both to maximize the productivity of the workout and to minimize the risk of injury.
  1. Select a weight that you think would be maintanable for serveral reps
  2. Lie flat on the bench and find a spot where the bar will be above your pectoral muscles when your arms are straightened
  3. Grip the bar with a width a fair bit wider than that of your shoulders
  4. Ensure your feet are flat on the ground and that you will be balanced when lifting
  5. Gently take the bar off of the weight rack and lower it just above your chest, while allowing your elbows to bend to the sides
  6. Keep steady as you push the bar back up to the point where your arms are almost straightened (but not fully)
  7. Repeat this process for around 10 reps and up to 3 sets of 10

Variations

  • Narrow grip: Works the inner part of the pecs
  • Incline: Works the upper part of the pecs
  • Decline: Works the lower part of the pecs
