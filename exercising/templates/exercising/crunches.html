Exercise Tips - Crunches

Synopsis

Crunches are a staple exercise for anyone seeking to improve their core muscles to achieve a six pack, promote
good posture, or reduce back pain. It is important to have good core muscles as they help provide a foundation for
most other exercises.

The Basics

Everyone has a slightly different way to do crunches, but there are few consistencies that should be met. As you want
to work out your ab muscles, you should try to avoid straining your neck too much as that detracts from the exercise
itself and can lead to discomfort. Additionally, you should try to maintain tension throughout the whole exercise, where
you sit up to a level where you still feel tension and lay down not completely flat, but just enough to still feel the strain.
  1. Find a mat to lay down on and bend your knees while your feet are flat on the ground
  2. Contract your ab muscles so that your back lifts off the ground a couple of inches, while
    keeping your head and neck relatively still.
  3. At the top, make sure to squeeze your abs to really feel the burn when they're activated
  4. Slowly lower back down to your starting position where you maintain tension
  5. Repeat this as many times as you can do so while maintaining proper form

Variations

There are different types of crunches that work different parts of the ab muscles. A few are listed below:
  • Standard crunch: Outlined above
  • Frog crunch: Crunches where your feet are together and at a 45 degree angle to the ground
  • Vertical leg crunch: Crunches where your feet are pointed up to the ceiling. These are good
    at engaging your back muscles at the same time.
  • Reverse crunch: Instead of lifting your torso up, you keep it still and rather lift your legs up
    to point up to the ceiling.
