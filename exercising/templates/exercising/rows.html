Exercise Tips - Rows

Synopsis

Rows are a good workout for strengthening your upper and lower back muscles, in addition to a few others in your
arms, and your core muscles. As you may be able to guess, it simulates the same kind of motion that you would get
from rowing a boat.

The Basics

Rows are a good "pull" exercise, in comparison to the many other common "push" exercises mentioned here and elsewhere.
It is an important exercise to help provide further stability to your core by strenghening your back muscles. Many variations
on the rows exercise exist, so the guideline below is for one-arm dumbbell rows.
  1. Select a weight that you think would be maintanable for around 10 reps
  2. Stand with your feet about hip-width apart
  3. Stand in a lunge position and lean forward with your back foot being straightened
  4. Place the arm that is not lifting on your thigh or on a bench to keep balance
  5. While ensuring your core is steady, keep your elbow close to your ribs when youre holding the weight
  6. Slowly lower the weight by moving only your shoulder blades until your arm is fully extended
  7. Drive your elbow upwards while still keeping your elbow close to your ribs until the weight gets to chest level
  8. At this point, really squeeze your back muscles so that you can really feel them engaged
  9. Repeat this about 10 times for 3 sets for each arm

Variations

There are many types of row exercises, which are including but not limited to:
  • Single Dumbbell: Using one dumbbell at a time to really feel the motion
  • Double Dumbbell: Using two dumbells at the same time to balance
  • Barbell: Balancing a barball across both hands and performing a similar motion to the dumbbells
  • Machine: Some machines have you sitting and rowing, which looks very similar to actual rowing
  • Resistance bands: It is also possible to use resistance bands to perform this exercise
    by sitting with your legs straight forward and having the band be held by your feet while you
    pull back on the band with both arms.
