Exercise Tips - Leg Press

Synopsis

The leg press exercise is an important exercise when you plan to work out your glutes, hamstrings, and quadriceps.
It is facilitated with a machine that is either horizontal or at a 45 degree angle.

The Basics

The leg press machine has a flat plate that you push against with your feet. The weight level is easily
customizable and so too are the specific muscles that you highlight. By moving your feet around the plate
you can focus more on calves or quads for example.
  1. Determine a weight that you can do comfortably and then lay your back and head flat against the seat
  2. Place your feet in the middle of the plate and slowly push the plate until your legs are extended but
    your knees are not locked and your feet are flat. It is important to keep tension to maximize your workout
  3. Slowly reverse and contract your legs back to the starting position
  4. Repeat this for about 10 reps and 3 sets of that

Variations

There are a couple variations on squats that usually involve adding weights. A few are listed below:
  • Glut/Hamstring focused: If you place your feet higher up on the plate, you work these muscles more
  • Quads/Calves focused: If you place your feet lower on the plate, you work these muscles more
