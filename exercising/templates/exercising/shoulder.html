Exercise Tips - Shoulder press

Synopsis

The shoulder press or overhead press, is an exercise that focuses on several muscle groups such as the deltoids (shoulders)
pecs, triceps, and trapezius. As this exercise is usually performed while standing, you will also need to have good balance
and engage your core muscles to ensure you stay upright.

The Basics

With this exercise, it is especially important to select a weight that you find comfortable to do
as the weights will be going above your head when you lift. The shoulder press can also be done on
a machine, with a pair of dumbbells, or a barbell.
  1. Select a weight that you think would be maintanable for around 10 reps
  2. Stand with your feet about shoulder-width apart
  3. Bring the weights to a level around your collarbone and just in front of your neck
  4. While ensuring you're steady and standing up straight, push the weight up and move your neck forward a bit
  5. Slowly lower the weight while simultaneously tiling your head back to make space for the weights
  6. Repeat this about 10 times for 3 sets

Variations

There are a couple types of shoulder press, which are including but not limited to:
  • Dumbbell: Using two dumbbells with one in each hand
  • Barbell: Balancing a barball across both hands
  • Resistance bands: It is also possible to use resistance bands to perform this exercise
    by standing on the middle of the band and pushing up while the band is taut.
