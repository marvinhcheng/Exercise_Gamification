Exercise Tips - Squats

Synopsis

Squats are simple and easy exercise to work out various muscles in your legs, such as the hips, glutes and thighs.

The Basics

The simple squat can be done without any additional weights or equipment.
  1. Place your feet about shoulder width apart with a slight bend in your knees
  2. Squat down to a level about where you would be sitting in a chair. This is about when
    your thighs are parallel to the ground. Make sure to not let your knees protrude beyond
    your feet or otherwise you'd be using your knees.
  3. Pause at this "sitting position" for a moment
  4. Contract your glutes and hamstrings to lift out of this position to return back to the starting position
  5. Repeat this as many times as you can do so while maintaining proper form

Variations

There are a couple variations on squats that usually involve adding weights. A few are listed below:
  • Standard squat: Outlined above
  • Dumbbell squat: Similar to above, but you hold a dumbbell at chest level
  • Sumo squat: This squat involves having a wider stance with your feet pointing
    outwards, which engages the inner thigh more.
  • Barbell squat: The commonly seen form of squats with weights involved where the
    bar rests on the shoulders and behind the neck.
